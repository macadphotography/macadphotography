There is a particular hour, just after first light, when a city belongs to no
one. The shutters are still down, the coffee is only beginning to brew, and the
streets carry the kind of silence you can photograph.

![Empty street at first light](01.jpg "First light")

I like to walk without a plan on mornings like this. The camera stays at my hip
and I let the city set the pace. A cyclist here, a shopkeeper unlocking a grille
there — the ordinary choreography of a place waking up.

![A shopkeeper opening up](02.jpg "The first customer")

By the time the crowds arrive, the spell is broken and the city becomes itself
again: loud, fast, wonderful. But for that first hour, it is quiet enough to
listen to.

![Light spilling across a quiet square](03.jpg "Before the crowds")
